Mollweide projection

This projection fills an ellipse with axes in proportion 2:1. It solves partially a problem with all cylindrical projections (such as Plate-Carrée, Mercator, Gall-Peters and others), namely that the poles are not displayed at one unique location. North pole on Plate-Carrée corresponds to the whole upper border of the map rectangle. Mercator projection is even worse, since the poles are represented at infinity. This distorts hugely polar regions, such as the Antarctica. Mollweide projections solves this and reduces the distortion.

If you test some Tissot's ellipses, you can observe that Mollweide projection is equal-area, that is, the areas of regions are preserved (hence all Tissot's ellipses have the same area). Also, its rounded shape reminds the sphericity of the Earth, which is not apparent on other squared maps.
