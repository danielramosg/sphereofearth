Mollweide projection

This projection fills an ellipse with axes in proportion 2:1. It partially solves a problem with all cylindrical projections (such as Plate-Carrée, Mercator, Gall-Peters and others), namely that the poles are not displayed at one unique location. The North pole on a Plate-Carrée projection corresponds to the whole upper border of the map rectangle. The Mercator projection is even worse, since the poles are represented at infinity. This hugely distorts polar regions, such as Antarctica. Mollweide projection solves this and reduces the distortion.

If you test some Tissot's ellipses, you can observe that the Mollweide projection is an equal-area projection, that is, the areas of regions are preserved (hence all Tissot's ellipses have the same area). Also, its rounded shape reminds us the sphericity of the Earth, which is not apparent on other squared maps.
